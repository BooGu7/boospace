## FAQ

### **1. Is Cal.com really open-source?**
Yes! You can self-host Cal.com using their [GitHub repository](https://github.com/calcom/cal.com). The open-source version includes all core features, though the managed cloud plan adds extras like analytics and priority support.

### **2. Can I embed Cal.com into my SaaS product?**
Absolutely. Cal.com provides a **full API** and embeddable widgets, making it easy to integrate scheduling directly into your app. Many SaaS products use it for booking flows.

### **3. How does round-robin scheduling work?**
Round-robin scheduling automatically distributes bookings across a team (e.g., sales reps, support agents). When a customer books a meeting, Cal.com assigns it to the next available team member in rotation, ensuring fair workload distribution.

### **4. Does Cal.com work with Google Calendar?**
Yes! Cal.com syncs **two-way** with Google Calendar (and Outlook). Any changes in Cal.com update your Google Calendar, and vice versa, so you never miss a booking.

### **5. What’s the difference between Cal.com and Calendly?**
Cal.com is **open-source, self-hostable, and developer-friendly**, while Calendly is a **proprietary, cloud-only** tool. Cal.com offers advanced features like round-robin scheduling, collective bookings, and a full API—making it better for teams and developers.

### **6. Can I use Cal.com for free?**
Yes! The **open-source version is free** (self-hosted). Cal.com also offers a **Pro plan ($9/month)** for team features and a **Business plan ($29/month)** for advanced scheduling.

### **7. Is Cal.com good for freelancers?**
Yes, especially if you need:
- Custom booking forms (e.g., payment integration).
- Automated follow-ups (pre/post-meeting emails).
- White-label branding (no Calendly link in your booking page).

### **8. How secure is Cal.com?**
Since it’s open-source, you can **audit the code yourself**. For self-hosted instances, security depends on your setup. The managed cloud version includes enterprise-grade security features like SSO and audit logs.

### **9. Can I migrate from Calendly to Cal.com?**
Yes! Cal.com provides **migration tools** to import your Calendly events, users, and settings. The process is straightforward if you’re self-hosting.

### **10. What’s the biggest downside of Cal.com?**
The **learning curve**—it’s more powerful but requires more setup than Calendly. If you’re not comfortable with self-hosting or APIs, it might feel overwhelming.
